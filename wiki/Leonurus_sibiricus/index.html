Medicinal uses(Warning!)

Chinese motherwort is unusual amongst Chinese herbs in that it is often prescribed for use on its own and not in a mixture with other plants[6]. The whole plant is antibacterial, antispasmodic, astringent, cardiac, depurative, diaphoretic, diuretic, emmenagogue, hypnotic, nervine, oxytocic, stomachic, tonic, uterine stimulant[7][8][9][5][10][11][12][13][14]. The seeds have a similar action to the plant, but are a less effective diuretic and depurative[6]. The plant is used in the treatment of painful and excessive menstruation, post-partum bleeding, oedema, kidney complaints, kidney stones, eczema and abscesses[6][15]. A tincture is used in the treatment of rheumatic fever[13]. The plant stimulates uterine contractions and should not therefore be used when in the earlier stages of pregnancy[15]. The plant is harvested when in flower but before the seeds have set, and is dried for later use[6]. The plant contains about 0.05% of an alkaloid called leonurine[15]. This has a curare-like effect on the motor-endings of the nervous system, acting in small doses as a stimulant to the respiratory system but in large doses causing respiratory paralysis[15].

Propagation

Seed - sow late spring in a cold frame. When they are large enough to handle, prick the seedlings out into individual pots and plant them out in the summer.

Cultivation

Prefers a well-drained moist soil in sun or partial shade[6]. Prefers a poor soil[16].

Plants are hardy to at least -15°c[6].

There is some confusion over the correct name for this species, it should quite probably be called L. japonicus.
